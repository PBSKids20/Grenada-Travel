Grenada History

Grenada's first Inhabitants

The first inhabitants of Grenada were the Carib Indians whom were living peacefully with nature before the Europeans arrived.

Colonization of Grenada

Colonization started when Christopher Columbus arrived on his third trip to the "new-world" in 1498. Grenada remained uncolonized for more than 100 years after its discovery; early English efforts to settle the island were unsuccessful. In 1650, a French company founded by Cardinal Richelieu purchased Grenada from the English and established a small settlement.

Grenada's Independence

In the general election of August 1967, the Grenada United Labour Party defeated the Grenada National Party and took office under the premiership of Eric M. Gairy, a trade unionist. Grenada became an independent nation on February 7, 1974.
